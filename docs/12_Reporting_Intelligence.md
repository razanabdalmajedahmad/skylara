# Chapter 24: Reporting & Analytics System
## 24.1 Reporting Architecture — The Reality Check
Most startup reporting is a joke: SELECT * with no indexes, no caching, no pre-aggregation. At 500 DZs × 200 jumpers/day, that's 100K bookings/day. Your reporting will kill your database unless you architect for scale.
SkyLara's dual-layer system separates operational queries from historical analytics. Real-time dashboards query Redis + read replicas. Historical reports use pre-computed aggregation tables refreshed nightly. The ReportEngine routes requests intelligently: cache → read replica → aggregation table → compute.
### Dual-Layer Architecture
Query routing logic: check Redis first (cache hit = instant). Miss? Read replica (never primary). For aggregation queries, go straight to daily_revenue_summary. For edge cases (last 2 hours of data), union read replica + today's aggregates.

# CHAPTER 25: OPERATIONAL INTELLIGENCE UPGRADE
The intelligence layer transforms SkyLara from a transaction processor into a decision support system. Every action—booking, jump, cancellation, equipment maintenance—feeds an event-sourced pipeline that generates real-time insights, predictive alerts, and optimization recommendations. This chapter describes the architecture, algorithms, and implementation details for operational, predictive, safety, financial, and growth intelligence.
## 25.1 Intelligence Architecture
The intelligence pipeline is event-driven: every DZ action becomes an intelligence event consumed by dedicated workers.
Event Pipeline Flow:
Action → Event Bus: Booking confirmed, load filled, instructor logged in, equipment inspected, incident reported
Event Bus → Intelligence Workers (BullMQ): fanout to specialized workers by intelligence category
Workers → Insights Store (Redis + MySQL): compute metrics, cache hot data, persist historical analytics
Insights Store → Dashboard Push (Socket.IO): push updated metrics to connected DZ operators in <5 seconds

## 25.5 Dynamic Pricing Intelligence
Base price is modified in real-time based on demand, temporal factors, passenger characteristics, and inventory. The system ensures margin health while optimizing conversion.
A/B Testing:
For high-value updates to pricing rules, the system can A/B test two price points across randomly assigned loads. After sufficient volume (100+ bookings per variant), calculate lift in conversion, total revenue, and margin impact.
## 25.6 No-Show & Cancellation Intelligence
A no-show prediction model scores every booking 0-100 on likelihood of non-appearance. High-risk bookings trigger preventive actions: deposit requirement, extra reminders, or tactical overbooking.
## 25.7 Weather Impact Intelligence
Real-time weather integration feeds activity-specific hold thresholds (tandems 14 kts, AFF 18 kts, experienced 25 kts). The system predicts holds 30+ minutes ahead and estimates revenue at risk.
Historical Pattern Detection:
Analyze last 90 days of weather holds by hour and day-of-week. Example: 'This DZ has weather holds 35% of afternoons in July—recommend 65% of summer loads in morning slots.'
## 25.8 Safety Intelligence
Incident tracking, pattern detection, and anomaly alerting keep safety metrics visible. The system calculates incident rates per 1,000 jumps, detects clusters, and flags equipment compliance issues.
## 25.9 Growth & Retention Intelligence
Track jumper lifecycle—first visit → repeat → regular → power user—and flag churn risk. Automated win-back campaigns target at-risk jumpers with personalized offers.
## 25.10 Financial Intelligence
Continuous revenue optimization identifies underperforming time slots, activities, and pricing tiers. Cost analysis ensures each jump margin is healthy. Payout anomalies are flagged for manual review.
## 25.11 Cross-DZ Intelligence (Platform-Level)
Global benchmarking ranks DZs by revenue, safety, and utilization. Seasonal migration patterns show which jumpers travel between DZs, enabling cross-promotions. Underserved regions are identified for expansion recommendations.
## 25.12 Intelligence Dashboard & Alerts
DZ operators receive a daily intelligence briefing at 7am with yesterday's summary, today's forecast, and top 3 actionable recommendations. Real-time alerts are triggered by configurable rules and pushed across multiple channels.
